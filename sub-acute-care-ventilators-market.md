<p><p>The Sub-acute Care Ventilators market is divided into two primary types: invasive and non-invasive. Invasive sub-acute care ventilators involve direct airway management through intubation or tracheostomy, providing controlled ventilation for patients with severe respiratory issues. Conversely, non-invasive sub-acute care ventilators support breathing through masks or nasal interfaces, offering a less intrusive option for patients with moderate respiratory distress. Both types are essential for managing respiratory support in sub-acute care settings, addressing different patient needs and comfort levels.</p></p>
