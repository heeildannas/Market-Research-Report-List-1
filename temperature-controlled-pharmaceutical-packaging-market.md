<p><p>The temperature-controlled pharmaceutical packaging market encompasses three key applications: frozen, chilled, and ambient. Frozen packaging is essential for biologics and certain vaccines that require storage below -18°C to maintain efficacy. Chilled packaging, typically between 2°C to 8°C, is used for medications like insulin and some biotechnology products, ensuring stability. Ambient packaging, maintained at room temperature, accommodates a broad range of pharmaceuticals that do not need specific temperature conditions, maximizing accessibility and convenience for distribution and storage.</p></p>
