<p><p>The dry chickpeas market primarily consists of two main types: Kabuli chickpeas and Desi chickpeas. Kabuli chickpeas are larger, lighter in color, and have a smooth skin, predominantly favored in Western cuisines for their mild flavor and tender texture. In contrast, Desi chickpeas are smaller, darker, and have a rougher texture, commonly used in South Asian dishes. Both types are rich in protein and fiber, catering to distinct culinary preferences and nutritional demands in global markets.</p></p>
