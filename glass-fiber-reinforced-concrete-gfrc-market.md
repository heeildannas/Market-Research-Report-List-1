<p><p>Glass Fiber Reinforced Concrete (GFRC) is a composite material that combines concrete with glass fibers to enhance its strength, flexibility, and durability. The GFRC market is categorized into three main types: sprayed, premix, and hybrid. </p><p>Sprayed GFRC is applied using a spray technique for intricate designs. Premix GFRC incorporates glass fibers during mixing, offering uniform strength throughout. Hybrid GFRC combines both methods, leveraging the benefits of each to produce versatile products suitable for various architectural and structural applications.</p></p>
